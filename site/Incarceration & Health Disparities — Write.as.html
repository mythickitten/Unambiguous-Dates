Unambiguous Dates!

Tired of confusing date and time formats? Me too!

Hover over in-text citations to view complete citations. Click a citation to visit the link in the citation.

You check a patient's chart, and the most recent physical examination was conducted at '4/6 2019'. When was it? The 6th of April or the 4th of June? And what about that last number? Is that the year? The time? Something else? We may never know because this writer used an ambiguous date format!

While it's fun to joke about these kinds of small misunderstandings here, it can cause serious problems in the real world! Say your boss emails you requesting to meet on 8/7 for Coffee. Today is the 7th of July. Do you meet them tomorrow or a month from now?

The Solution

It's incredibly simple actually; just write the name of the month out! Poof, ambiguity gone!

Instead of writing '9/10', write out '9 October' or 'September 10'.

If you're tight on space, it's okay to use the three-letter abbreviation for the month like '1 Dec' or '6 Feb'.

Wait, what about the time?

Time formats can be just as ambiguous but are just as easy to fix; just write a colon to clearly signifiy that it is a time and not the year, a price, a measurement or anything else!

Instead of writing '2019', write '20:19'.
